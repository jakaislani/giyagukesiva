    ➡ Sam Morril Biography

    Sam Morril is an American stand-up comedian, actor, writer, and producer. Sam was born in Chelsea, although he grew up in New York City. Morril is one of the fastest-rising stand-up comics in New York City and one of the best joke writers in the scene today. For the audience who loves him for his funny stand-up comedy, we’ve some unknown facts about him that you will surely love to read.

    8 Things You Didn’t Know About Sam Morril

    1. Early Years Of His Life

    He attended Browning School in Manhattan’s Upper Eastside. Sam’s parents divorced when he was a little child, so he was raised by a loving stepfather and mother. His stepfather, a well-known attorney in the publishing industry, gave him the name Morril. His mother, Marilyn Greenberg, is a painter. [1]

    2. Educational History

    He graduated from Tulane University in New Orleans after attending the Browning School in Manhattan.

    3. Struggling Time

    Morril was named one of Comedy Central’s “Comics to Watch” in 2011 and has collaborated with Dave Attell, Marc Maron, Conan O’Brien, and Amy Schumer, among others. He interned on The Colbert Report before appearing on The Late Show with Stephen Colbert in April 2016. He competed on America’s Got Talent in 2016 with his stand-up routine but did not win. [2]

    4. His Famous Releases

    In September 2018, Comedy Central aired his first one-hour special, Positive Influence. In February 2020, his second one-hour special was released on Comedy Central’s YouTube channel. Morril’s third Comedy Central stand-up special, “I Got This,” was released on their YouTube channel and received over 1.5 million views in the first two weeks. [3]

    5. Getting To The Top Gradually

    In the DC thriller movie “Joker,” he made a brief cameo appearance as a comic at an open mic night (2019). He and comedian Mark Normand co-host the weekly podcast “We Might be Drunk.”

    6. His Love Life

    Morril has been in a relationship with fellow comedian Taylor Tomlinson since March 2020.

    7. A Successful Show Of His Own

    Morril hosted his program on MSG, “People Talking Sports,” which lasted for two seasons and was nominated for an Emmy. Joker, a movie that is up for an Oscar, features an appearance by Morril as well.

     8. A Lively Person

    A mixture of jokes, positive energy, and humor make Morril. He became well-known for his black humor on comedy shows. He stands out from most comedians thanks to his particular delivery style and inventive use of punchlines. Years have passed before he received attention.
